About


I've always been fascinated by breaking things down, understanding their fundamental parts, then rebuilding them in my own way to create something new. This desire to get 'under the hood', coupled with a long standing curiosity about computer programming, led me to Harvard's CS50x which I began in 2024.

Coming from an extensive background in audio engineering and musical composition, I found the transition into programming exciting and intuitive. Already familiar with the harmony between technical proficiency and artistic expression, I became particualrly hooked on website design.

I built a number of browser based programs including a stock managemnt app that my colleagues used to speed up a laborious and error-prone process that was currently in place. On top of it's functionality and clear user-interface, I also designed it to fit the company's branding and visual identity.

I was then approached by friends and colleagues to build their small business websites, either coded completely from scratch with custom grahics and interactive elements, or to help fix and update their half-finished work on various CMS including Wix, Squarespace, Shopify, and Wordpress, which I picked up quickly.

I recently began the Full Stack Open course from University of Helsinki covering React, Node.js, MongoDB, GraphQL and TypeScript to further expand my abilities.

wyattaudiouk.com


For this project, the client wanted a custom website that would not only advertise their range of services, but also convey a strong and unique aesthetic.

This served as an invitation into their world, strengthening their brand identity and ultimately leading to a consistently expanding and loyal customer base.







I shot and edited a showreel style video of the client at work to use as a landing page background.

Then, using various responsive layouts, modals, and scroll-driven animations, I created an intuitive and engaging user experience.







Finally, I came up with the idea to have an interactive element that would provide an enjoyable and memorable experience for the user.

This further showcased the clients personality and interests, providing a deeper connection with their customers' demographic.




quakingaspensmusic.com


This artist wanted a simple and effective platform to showcase their work and provide information in a unique and central location.

Being fast and accesible to both desktop and mobile browsers was paramount so I made good use of media queries, content placeholders, and web optimised media, along with SEO optimization and accessibility considerations.

As the sites primary users are both fans and music industry members alike, the information had to be both clear and easy to access, as well as being consistent with the artists aesthetic.




jjnexus.com


As both a Sound Designer and Audio Engineer, this client wanted a website that could showcase their services in both of these fields, side by side, and in a manner consistent with those fields respective common practices.


Leaning into a game-like aesthetic, I sourced various pixel based components and created interactive animated elements.



stone-swords.com


The aim for this electronic musicians website was to say as much as possible with as little as possible. I designed a minimal, one page website that feels both ancient and modern, simple at first, but whose secrets may be revealed to those who are drawn to seek them.

Care was taken to ensure a cohesive experience across all devices and screen dimensions.



velkndstudio.com
